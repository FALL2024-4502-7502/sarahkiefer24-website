10 Best Study Spots

Here you can find my suggestions for the best study spots on campus, with a mix of quiet and busy places to fit your ideal work environment!

ACOLA Coffee

Visit ACOLA Coffee

300 N 10th St, Columbia MO, 65201

(573) 447-8050

ACOLA Coffee brings the outside in with a plant oasis while you study. The plethora of options from tea to coffee and scones that always look delicious, Acola is the spot to hunker down on those reading assignments or get the tedious work out of the way if it’s a little louder of an atmosphere.

Sage

Visit Sage

11 N 9th St, Columbia, MO 65201

(573) 449-8215

Sage is a welcoming space, also located in downtown Columbia, that brings together comfort and class. The tables are set up in a booth style lending options for a long study session and the sweet treats make for an enjoyable experience with lots of fun coffee flavors as well.

Lakota Coffee

Visit Lakota Coffee

24 S. 9th St. Columbia, MO 65201

(573) 874-2852

Lakota Coffee has quality coffee with a lower price tag than some of the other downtown coffee shops. The space is a little less of a homey feel than Fretboard, but it’s still a great place to study while indulging your caffeine habit. Lakota constantly has coupons and deals on the app as well.

Fretboard Coffee

Visit Fretboard Coffee

1013 E Walnut St #10

(573) 227-2233

Fretboard Coffee is a quaint, almost hidden coffee shop in Downtown Columbia. You access it by going down the staircase immediately to the right of Artlandish Art Gallery. There are sofas, chairs, and lamps that create a comfortable and relaxing space to spend hours getting work done.

Peace Park

Visit Peace Park

401 S 9th St, Columbia, MO 65201

(573) 884-6307

Peace Park is a fitting name for this peaceful place that encompasses a park in the middle of campus. College students walk their dogs throughout the park, creating a fun people-watching place as well as a perfect spot to read while getting in some vitamin D.

Ellis Library

Visit Ellis Library

104 Ellis Library, 520 S 9th St, Columbia, MO 65211

(573) 882-4701

Ellis library is a go-to spot for students to study in a guaranteed quiet space. The library gets quitter as you go up each level, so the 2nd floor and up are ideal for reading assignments. Ellis library has plenty of seating options and computers available for use.

Bond Life Sciences Center

Visit Bond Life Sciences Center

1201 Rollins St, Columbia, MO 65211

(573) 882-0093

The Bond Life Sciences Center has several different floors of chairs, loungers, tables, whiteboards, and more that create an ergonomic sitting environment that encourages learning in a relatively quiet space.

La Calle Café

Visit La Calle Café

214 South 8th Street Columbia, MO 65201

(573) 825-8960

La Calle Café is the perfect place to study in a quiet, downtown location. There is limited seating, so keep that in mind when checking it out during busy times, but the plethora of handmade drinks, pastries, and sandwiches are delectable and never fail to curb my cravings.

Aroma Coffee House

Visit Aroma Coffee House

1009 N Providence Rd, Columbia, MO 65201

(573) 228-9480

Aroma Coffee House is located close to but not on campus. It offers an off-campus coffee shop feel that usually isn’t as busy as some of the downtown shops. They have a great avocado toast!

Journalism School Library

Visit the Journalism School Library

401 S 9th St, Columbia, MO 65211

(573) 882-75023

The Journalism School Library is an obvious choice if you are looking to make the most of a couple hours or minutes in between classes. With an entirely quiet environment, you can get a lot of work done in a short amount of time and browse their resources as well – like the magazine or newspaper archives if you need examples for class projects.
