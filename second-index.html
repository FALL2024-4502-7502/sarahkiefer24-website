7 Best Beach Reads

These are some of the best books to read on the beach or curled up on the couch on a vacation with nothing but time on your hands. Mainly aimed towards romance novels, these books are easy reads that can take you off the screens and into the pages.

Image credit

Meet Me At the Lake

By Carley Fortune

This lakeside romance is one that will have you waiting and wondering the whole way through. A 20-minute conversation with an artist had Fern Brookbanks hooked, so much so that she thought about that encounter for many years after which. Read what happens when the same artist arrives practically on her doorstep.

The People We Meet on Vacation

By Emily Henry

A romance novel about the time in which two friends, Alex and Poppy, decided to try out being in a relationship – with each other. I know, imagine how that can end? But was it really that bad or did they actually have too many things in common and just couldn’t get past the shock?

Image credit

Red, White, and Royal Blue

By Casey McQuiston

Prince Henry and Alex Clare-mont Diaz clash in the beginning, but when you keep your friends close and your enemies closer you might just become more than friends in the end. The two replicate a sort-of Romeo and Juliet fantasy world in this easy-to-read romance novel.

Book summary >>

Image credit

Swan Song

By Elin Hilderbrand

This subtle Nantucket novel best read on a long beach day with ample time to digest the drama and happenings of the plot line. Colleen Coyle, or better known as ‘Coco’ gets swept up in the melodrama in more ways than one and finds herself in a missing person and house on fire type of situation – in a literal sense.

Book summary >>

Image credit

Part of Your World

By Abby Jimenez

Caught between legacy and romance, Alexis Montgomery finds that a grilled cheese is the way to her heart and a simple life is what she was looking for all along- despite her family’s apprehension.

Book summary >>

Image credit

Heart Principle

By Helen Hoang

Anna Sun gives up her dream of playing violin to take a small break and see if she can get her magic back. When she goes for a simpler, less complicated relationship in her life to make things easier, she finds that she doesn’t know if that is still the best approach.

Book summary >>

Image credit

One Italian Summer

By Rebecca Serle

Katy grapples with her mother’s death and to make matters worse she feels like she has to go on the trip of a lifetime that was supposed to be a mother-daughter getaway. Instead, everywhere she looks in Positano, Italy reminds her of her mother – and some resemblances stick with her more than others.
